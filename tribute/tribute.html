A tribute to a brillant mentor and teacher

You were one of the first faculty members I met with when I came to our institution in 2016. Our mentoring relationship began immediately. You were a catalyst for career success, not just for me but for so many others. You have taught me so many things. The first lesson was about saying yes. Today, there seems to be a big focus on when to say no. Instead, you taught me early on to say yes, and it has opened countless doors in my career.When our institution was selected to send a small team to present a major education grant proposal in person, most universities sent deans. However, you advocated to send three very junior faculty members including me. We were specifically asked at the grant review where you were and where our dean was. We simply stated that you believed in us, and we were there to represent our institution—and we came home with the grant. This is not the only grant you were responsible for; you participated in and facilitated grant funding from countless foundations and national funding organizations. Your work is nothing short of extraordinary!

Thank you for being there, for showing me the path. Thank you for always caring and giving it all you have. Thank you for not running when I needed your help, and thank you for always caring about my health. Thank you for always staying by my side and giving me the courage and the pride to do things I’ve never done before. Thank you for giving me something to stay alive for. Thank you for always loving me and adoring my talents and skills. Thank you for always being there, for all of the sad times and the thrills. Thank you for always telling me what’s wrong and what’s right, and thank you for helping me on the path to life. Thank you my Mentor!
